#Departments

# Synopsis:
The department responsible for researching [[Anomaly Overview|anomalies]] and their properties, both in order to correctly contain them, and for the wider benefit of the foundation. 

Two types of anomalies are researched within the department, the [[Pre-Made Anomalies]] and the [[Procedurally Generated Anomalies]]. (see the [[Research Department#Research:|Research]] section for differences in how they are managed). This is used to provide more variety in gameplay between the anomalies staff will be aware of and the ones brought by the [[Anomaly Recovery Squadron]] for categorisation.

# List of Roles:
All roles, with exception of Research Operations Director, are lab specific, tasked with researching a specific anomaly at a time.

- Command: Research Operations Director (Level 4-(Command), All Lab Accesses + Containment Lab Accesses)

- Research Lead (Level 3-4, All Lab X Accesses + Containment Lab X Accesses)
- Senior Researcher (Level 3-4, Senior Lab X Accesses)
- Researcher (Level 2-3, Lab X Accesses)
- Junior Researcher (Level 1-2, Junior Lab X Accesses)

# Breaches:
Within breaches, scientists act as sheltered personnel, under the protection of the [[Security Department]] and [[External Roles##Mobile Task Force:|Mobile Task Force Agents]]. However, due to their closer connection to the anomalous, it is likely that some or many of the research team will either die or turn to unlawfully use the anomalies to their own benefit. As such, already contained anomalies, along with those being studied, should have some sort of use in the event of a breach.

# Research:
Research comes in two forms: the purely Roleplay style research, mostly reserved for the pre-made anomalies which couldn't reasonably researched in a purely mechanical sense, and there is a more mechanically driven research style, where various readings are taken, and stimuli applied to see how the anomaly acts, this leads into categorisation and exportation later down the line.

## Roleplay Style Research:
The roleplay style research relies more on the interactions between the Research or Security staff running the experiment, the [[D-Class and Related|D-Class]] participating, and the anomaly itself.
The benefits of this sort of testing is mostly for roleplay purposes, where each character involved is able to interact with the testing on a deeper level, where the reactions from the anomalies (either hard-coded or done by a character choosing it as a role) are more intricate, in order for the story told to be more cinematic/entertaining.
Notes done on the experiment are either done through a standard sheet, where the researchers write the conditions and results of the tests in their desired detail, or through voice recordings, allowing for a more reactionary style of categorisation; this also gives a good potential for these reports to be taken by command or more senior staff for purposes of [[Redaction]], also allowing for an [[Amnesticisation]] storyline.

## Mechanically Driven Research:
The mechanically driven research is designed as the most common form of research, to meet quotas of the amount of anomalies that must be categorised and sent off to other Foundation facilities.
The benefits of this sort of testing is a more surprising and unique experience, along with the presentation of actual research on an anomaly, with readings and criteria for reactions being tested. This also feeds into the main skill-based part of the research side of the department, where more experienced staff should be able to recognise how the type of anomaly is likely to react in specific ways. (Note that these are not even necessarily SCP items, simply anomalies originating from [[The Periphery]] that are being initially categorised).
Notes done on the experiment are designed to fit within a very strict format, based on specific multiple choice answers, or [[Anomalous Fields|readings]] from specific devices. Though more general notes or vocal records are permitted for in the moment notation. These specifically formatted notes are then sent off to the outer Foundation, alongside the anomaly itself in [[Research Department#Procedurally Generated Anomaly Containment|containment]] through an [[Delivery and Export System|automatic drone export system.]] (The same way deliveries will be made (Though it's probably easier to just fake it somehow)).
# Containment:
For both the Pre-Made and Procedurally generated anomalies, containment is required to keep the anomaly safe to be within foundation custody, without endangering the lives of any personnel stationed nearby.
## Procedurally Generated Anomaly Containment:
To contain the procedurally generated anomalies, a standardised containment unit is required. Especially for the [[Procedurally Generated Anomalies##Low Severity Anomalies|Lower Level]] anomalies, many need to be contained and actual rooms for each are unreasonable to expect. Therefore, smaller containment units are used (similar to the upstream versions for artifacts); these smaller containment units would have to be in multiple varieties for varying levels of anomaly, along with the specific method required to contain that [[Procedurally Generated Anomalies#Anomaly Type:|type]] of anomaly.
This would allow for a more simple way of containing these anomalies, and easy transport of the anomalies when initial tests have concluded.
For [[Procedurally Generated Anomalies##High Severity Anomalies|Higher Level]] anomalies, a more advanced containment system would likely be needed, probably utilising several portable elements to be nearby the anomaly to allow for correct containment without activating any of the triggers, or to nullify the anomaly's response. (For example, the use of a containment unit AND someone dragging a Reality Anchor nearby to keep it stabilised).
## Pre-Made Anomaly Containment:
For pre-made anomalies, the specific containment criteria are likely to already be set up, with little need for further interaction except for repairs, especially for [[Anomaly Classification#Euclid|Euclid]] and [[Anomaly Classification#Keter|Keter]] anomalies contained at the site. 
This also allows for specific anomalies to have higher standards of containment, where both the [[Engineering Department|engineering]] and security departments must spend some of their time dealing with the anomaly's containment to preserve it; this could also be improved through the use of an alert system, especially one that can be subverted by sentient anomalies.

## Making Containment Not Boring:
One of the main aspects of containment, for both forms of anomaly, is to prevent containment from ever becoming too boring. As such, the containment procedures that require constant surveillance or guarding should either be lessened to the containment being checked frequently, or completely removed to ensure it's actually fun to deal with. (This generally applies to Pre-Made Anomalies, as the procedurally generated anomalies are not intended for permeant containment at the facility).
