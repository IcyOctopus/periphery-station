# Subsection 1:

## Overview:
Violent offenses are defined by the harming, intentionally or through negligence, of another worker of the Foundation, without reasonable cause to do so. 
The punishments relevant to these offences are generally handled with greater severity, with the punishments proportional to both the significance and permanence of the action.
Additionally, the punishments of the crime may be mitigated through the motivations of the act, and the intent shown by the offender.
## Definitions:
An assault is considered as an action that causes minor direct harm, or causes the effected person to truly believe they are in imminent danger of being harmed. An assault can be punished with a {} level punishment.

Battery is considered an action that causes more significant direct harm. Battery can be punished with a {} level punishment.

Grievous Harm is considered the result of an action that causes significant direct harm, resulting in the effected person being rendered unconscious, and requiring medical attention. Grievous Harm can be punished with a {} level punishment.

Attempted Murder is considered an action that intends to lethally harm a person, or causing harm that would have been lethal without the provided medical attention. Attempted Murder can be punished with a {} level punishment, or the [[FLC Article 3#Subsection 5|non-lethal removal]] of the person or persons responsible.

Murder is considered an action that results in the death of a person, with more significant charges given according to the intent of the action, with a non-intentional murder considered with the same severity as a significant occasion of Grievous Harm. Intentional murder can be punished with a {} level punishment, or the [[FLC Article 3#Subsection 5|removal]] of the person or persons responsible.

# Subsection 2:

## Overview:
Damage To Assets is defined as an action that harms, intentionally or through negligence, Assets owned and maintained by the Foundation, without reasonable cause to do so.
The punishments relevant to these offences can be treated with either increased or lower significance, with the punishments proportional to the scale and replaceability of the assets damaged, along with the severity of the damage.
Reasonable cause for Damage To Access is significantly more difficult to be given, as most Foundation assets have no reason to be destroyed outside of [[FLC Article 5|Extraneous Circumstances]], therefore reasons will be more comprehensively stated in Article 5.
## Definitions:
Damage To Items is considered the result of an action that harms an item owned by the Foundation, where the item is of moderate to significant value, and where there is no reason for the damage to occur. Damage To Items can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment, scaled based on the significance of the item and damage.

Damage To Information is considered the result of an action that intentionally destroys a portion, or the entirety of a piece of information. Dependent on the importance and the access level of the information damaged. As this offence is unlikely to take place, and situations where it does is likely the result of intentional sabotage, Damage To Information can be punished by a level {} punishment.

Anomaly Damage is considered the result of an action, intentional or through negligence, that harms or destroys an anomaly under the care of the Foundation, without reasonable cause or permissions from relevant command. Anomaly Damage can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment, dependent on the severity of the damage.

Structural Damage is considered the result of an action that harms or destroys a portion of the Foundation site. Structural Damage can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment, according to the significance of the structure damaged, the level to which the damage occurred, alongside the difficulty for the damage to be repaired.

Containment Damage is considered the result of an action that, intentionally or through negligence, harms or completely destroys the containment of one or more anomalies. An unintentional occasion of containment damage may be treated as Structural Damage, based on how responsible the offender was for the action. Containment Damage can be punished with a {} level punishment, or the [[FLC Article 3#Subsection 5|removal]] of the person or persons responsible.

Vital Infrastructure Damage is considered the result of an action, intentional or through negligence, that causes damage to an item, machine or other part of infrastructure required for the safe and effective running of the station. This includes the server room, station generators, station life support and other infrastructure that could prevent the function of the station if compromised. If this offence was committed unintentionally, it may be treated as a severe case of structural damage.  Vital Infrastructure Damage can be punished with a {} level punishment, or the [[FLC Article 3#Subsection 5|removal]] of the person or persons responsible.

# Subsection 3:

## Overview:
Unlawful Access is defined as both the methods of, and the actual occasion of, intentionally gaining access into a restricted area, physical, recorded or digital, beyond the Foundation worker's provided accesses, without reasonable cause to do so.
The punishments relevant to this offenses are generally lower, due to the generally non-harmful nature of these actions, with the punishments scaled proportional to the access of the area accessed, and the methods used to access the area.
Reasonable cause for Unlawful Access is usually given through either permissions of a Foundation worker with lawful access to that location, some form of necessity for the access, or [[FLC Article 5|Extraneous Circumstances]].
## Definitions:
Trespassing is defined as the action of being within an area of an access level beyond the offender's without reasonable cause to do so. The punishment for this offense may scale on the specific significance of the area, along with the intent of the offender. Trespass may be punished with a level {} punishment.

Secure Trespassing is defined as the action of being within an area of an access level beyond the offender's, without reasonable cause to do so. The punishment for this offense may scale on the specific significance of the area. Specifically, Secure Trespass requires the area to be the access of either Command Staff, an area of Vital Infrastructure, or any area within the [[security department]], as a non-member. Secure Trespass may be punished with a level {} punishment.

Breaking And Entering is defined as the process of using destructive or subversive physical methods to gain entry and access into an area. The punishments for this offense scale on the security broken through and the significance of the damage caused. As such, Breaking And Entering can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment.

Unlawful Accesses are defined as gaining accesses to certain locations through methods that bypass or otherwise do not require a Foundation worker with lawfully acquired access to permit the relevant accesses. The punishments for this offense scale based on the security of the illegal accesses to locations or information gained. Therefore, Unlawful Accesses can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment. 

Hacking is defined as the use of digital or electronic methods to unlawfully access a physical or digital area through the manipulation and subversion of Foundation digital or electronic systems. The punishments for this offense scale based on both the potential damage caused to the system by the offender, along with the access gained. Hacking can be punished with a level {} to {} punishment.
